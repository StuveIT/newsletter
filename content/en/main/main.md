## News

- **Student services on the state election agenda**  
  State elections will be held in Baden-Württemberg on March 8, and the election campaign is in full swing. However, the topics of education and science are rarely mentioned. Yet there is much to be done in this area. The student services organizations ("Studierendenwerke") have drawn up election benchmarks for their area and listed the parties' responses. If there is not enough housing for students, cafeteria prices are rising, or the student services organizations are suffering from too much bureaucracy, this is due to decisions made by the state, and you can perhaps influence this with your vote.  
  (All German citizens over the age of 16 who are registered in Baden-Württemberg are eligible to vote.)
- **SOS-International Summit**   
  From March 18 to March 21, the SOS International Summit (Students Organizing for Sustainability) will take place in Konstanz. The summit is organized by the Green Office. The core theme of the summit, 'Student voices for a safe and just transition' aims to equip and empower students and staff to co-create, lead, and influence at their higher education institutions and beyond.

  You can find more information on the [SOS-International Summit](https://happeningnext.com/event/sos-international-summit-2026-eid1ef0kqesieba) website and on Instagram ([@greenofficekn](https://www.instagram.com/greenofficekn/)).
